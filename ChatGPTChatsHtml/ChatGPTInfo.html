Why is GPT4 proprietary when it comes from a company that prided itself on openess (OpenAI)?

ChatGPT


OpenAI initially prided itself on an ethos of openness, and indeed, its earlier models were released with fewer restrictions. However, as the models have become more powerful, OpenAI has increasingly faced a dilemma: the potential misuse of these models could have far-reaching negative implications, from generating misleading information or deepfakes to automating spam or malicious content.
 
In response to these challenges, OpenAI has taken a more cautious approach with the release of its larger models, such as GPT-3 and presumably GPT-4. This is part of their commitment to long-term safety and technical leadership, ensuring that AI benefits all of humanity. They aim to avoid enabling uses of AI that could harm humanity or unduly concentrate power.
 
While OpenAI continues to contribute to the AI community through research, papers, and smaller models, they have decided to restrict access to their larger models to control their usage better. They have developed APIs for these models that allow people to use the models without the ability to access the models directly, which gives OpenAI more ability to monitor and control how the models are used.
 
So, while the name "OpenAI" suggests complete openness, the organization's mission has evolved to balance openness with the need to prevent misuse of advanced AI technology. They still uphold the principle of broadly distributing benefits, but they do this now in a way that is more controlled and more mindful of the potential risks.
